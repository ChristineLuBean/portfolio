I make. I write. I hack.

Christine LuBean
writer, software engineer,
& ethical hacker

My love of solving problems and learning how things work led me to teaching myself how to create websites when I was 11. I went from a kid with a beaten up HTML manual to a full-stack developer over the course of my teenage and young adulthood years. When I was designing websites, I began keeping a personal blog, and this, along with my love of reading, inspired me to study English in college.

It wasn’t until after finishing my degree that I discovered a passion for cyber security. Although I did web development on the side, it was during the 2020 pandemic that I decided to go into tech full-time. My dad--who is a system administrator--found out I was finally fully committed to working in tech and began spamming me with articles about cyber security. While I dismissed cyber initially, after looking closer I discovered it was exactly what I wanted. It combines my love of tech with my passion for research and writing.

I'm an ethical hacker who wants to go beyond just preventing exploits. I want to improve the internet by securing webpages, applications, and teaching people how to be safe while they browse online.

When I’m not writing, coding, or hacking, I’m either creating digital illustrations, or I'm playing video games with my husband while being harassed by my two cats.
