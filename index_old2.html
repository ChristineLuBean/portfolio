I make. I write. I hack.

Christine LuBean
writer, software engineer,
& ethical hacker

My love of solving problems and learning how things work led me to teaching myself how to create websites when I was 11. I went from a kid with a beaten up HTML manual to a full-stack developer over the course of my teenage and young adulthood years. When I was designing websites, I began keeping a personal blog, and this, along with my love of reading, inspired me to study English in college.

It wasnâ€™t until after finishing my degree that I discovered a passion for cyber security. Although I did web development on the side, it was during the 2020 pandemic that I decided to go into tech full-time. My dad--who is a system administrator--found out I was finally fully committed to working in tech and began spamming me with articles about cyber security. While I dismissed cyber initially, after looking closer I discovered it was exactly what I wanted. It combines my love of tech with my passion for research and writing.

I'm an ethical hacker who wants to go beyond just preventing exploits. I want to improve the internet by securing webpages, applications, and teaching people how to be safe while they browse online. I believe that it is important to not only fix problems when they arise, but also to educate and prevent them from happening in the first place.
